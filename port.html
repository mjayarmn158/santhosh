Penetration Testing

Reconnaissance: Gathering information about the target network, systems, and applications.

Scanning: Identifying live hosts, open ports, and services running on the network.

Vulnerability Assessment: Detecting weaknesses and potential security flaws in the system.

Exploitation: Attempting to exploit identified vulnerabilities to gain unauthorized access.

Post-Exploitation: Assessing the extent of access gained and maintaining persistence.

Privilege Escalation: Gaining higher-level access or permissions within the system.

Web Application Testing: Evaluating web applications for common vulnerabilities like SQL injection and XSS.

Social Engineering: Testing human factors by simulating phishing or other social manipulation techniques.

Reporting: Documenting findings, vulnerabilities, and recommendations in a comprehensive report.

Remediation and Retesting: Assisting with fixing issues and verifying that vulnerabilities have been addressed effectively.
